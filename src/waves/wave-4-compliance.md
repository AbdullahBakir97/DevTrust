# Wave 4 — Compliance moat

**Months 9–12+ · "Sell to enterprises. Ride the regulatory wave."**

---

## What ships

| Product | Lane | Goal by end of wave |
|---|---|---|
| AgentGuard | Paid · Enterprise | Public launch, 3 paying teams, 1 SOC 2 Compliance Bundle sale |
| Dep Upgrade Pilot | Paid SaaS | Public launch, 10 paying teams (mix of platform and security buyers), $20K MRR |

By end of Wave 4: all 9 DevTrust products are live. Combined platform MRR target: $80K+. The wave's center of gravity is **enterprise sales**, not OSS launches — this is the most operationally different wave in the rollout.

## Why this order matters

Both Wave 4 products require credibility built up over the prior 9 months. Trying to sell either to a CISO or a security-conscious VP Engineering in Wave 1 would have failed because DevTrust didn't yet exist as a known brand. By Wave 4 we have:

- 4 OSS products with thousands of stars (community trust)
- 4 paid SaaS products with paying customers (commercial discipline)
- A SOC 2 audit underway or completed for our own platform (procurement readiness)

These three together are what AgentGuard's enterprise sales motion stands on.

## Month-by-month

### Month 10

- AgentGuard v0.5 closed beta with 5 design partner enterprises.
- Dep Upgrade Pilot v0.5 closed beta with 5 design partners (mix of platform engineering and security teams).
- SOC 2 Type 1 audit process completes (started in Wave 3).
- First enterprise sales hire (or partnership with a fractional GTM lead).

### Month 11

- AgentGuard public launch — but the launch is a sales motion, not an HN post. Press release, partner announcements with security tools (Snyk, Wiz, Lacework), CISO-targeted LinkedIn content.
- Dep Upgrade Pilot public launch — different motion: technical blog posts, security newsletter sponsorships, EU CRA preparation content.

### Month 12

- First enterprise contract signed for AgentGuard.
- Dep Upgrade Pilot at $20K+ MRR.
- One-year retrospective and Year 2 planning.

## Success criteria

- 9 of 9 DevTrust products live.
- Combined platform MRR > $80K.
- 3+ paying AgentGuard customers including 1 enterprise contract.
- 10+ paying Dep Upgrade Pilot customers.
- SOC 2 Type 1 attested. Type 2 in progress.

## Kill criteria

- AgentGuard 0 design partners after Wave 3 outreach → kill or push to Year 2; the enterprise sales motion needs a different shape than we have now.
- Dep Upgrade Pilot trial-to-paid conversion < 15% → reposition as security-only (drop the "AI-assisted upgrades" framing; lean compliance and risk).
- Combined platform MRR not > $60K by end of Wave 4 → hold all new product launches in Year 2 and focus on growing the existing 4 paid products.

## Headcount and resources

- 3–5 people total: 2 engineers, 1 GTM/sales lead, 1 part-time finance/operations support, founder.
- First full-time enterprise account executive (commission-based) hired in month 10 if pipeline data justifies.
- Tools budget: $2K/mo.
- Marketing budget: $8K/mo — LinkedIn ads, sponsored newsletters, conference sponsorships.
- Compliance budget: $15K-30K for SOC 2 audit and ongoing.

## GTM activities

- **Enterprise sales motion:**
  - Outbound prospecting via LinkedIn Sales Navigator, targeting CISOs at companies with known agent deployments.
  - Inbound capture of CTOs/VPEs reading our compliance content.
  - Demo-led sales process with proof-of-concept engagements.
- **Compliance-tailwind content:**
  - Weekly "EU CRA enforcement watch" newsletter (positions Dep Upgrade Pilot for the regulatory tailwind).
  - Quarterly "OWASP Top 10 for Agents — what we're seeing in production" report.
- **Partnership discussions:**
  - Snyk / Wiz integration partnership for Dep Upgrade Pilot.
  - GitGuardian integration for AgentGuard's secret-detection rules.
  - Vanta or Drata partnership for shared compliance evidence flow.

## Hand-off to Year 2

By end of Wave 4, the Year 2 entry conditions are:

- All 9 products in production with paying customers (or honestly killed by their kill-criteria).
- Combined MRR providing the team's runway.
- Wave 5 (Year 2) decisions: which products grow into platforms in their own right, which get sunsetted, what new wedges open up?
- Year 2 hypotheses to revisit: cross-product bundling pricing, second-mover regions (EMEA expansion, especially around CRA), new product additions if and only if the existing 9 are healthy.
